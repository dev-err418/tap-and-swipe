## How It Started

Marcus was a backend engineer at a logistics company. He built a simple habit tracker for himself because every app he tried was "either too complex or too ugly."

The first version took a weekend. It tracked three habits: water, exercise, and reading. That's it.

## The TikTok Moment

Six months after launch, Marcus posted a casual screen recording on TikTok showing his streak screen. It wasn't even meant to be promotional — he was showing a friend how his app worked.

The video got 2.3 million views in 48 hours.

> "I woke up to 40,000 downloads overnight. My $5/month Supabase instance was on fire." — Marcus Rivera

## Surviving the Spike

The first 24 hours were chaos. The backend couldn't handle the load. Push notifications stopped working. The App Store reviews tanked because of crashes.

What Marcus did:
1. **Migrated to Railway** for auto-scaling within hours
2. **Added a queue system** for push notifications with BullMQ
3. **Shipped a hotfix** that cached most data locally on-device
4. **Personally replied** to every 1-star review promising a fix

Within 72 hours, the app was stable again. Reviews recovered within two weeks.

## The Monetization Journey

Marcus tried three different monetization strategies:

**Attempt 1: Tip jar** — $47 total. "People don't tip for productivity apps."

**Attempt 2: Ads** — $800/month but terrible UX. "Users hated it and I hated building around ad SDKs."

**Attempt 3: Premium subscription** ($3.99/month) — The winner. Key insight: he made the free version limited to 3 habits, premium unlocked unlimited.

Current MRR: $28,000.

## The Feature That Changed Everything

Marcus added "accountability partners" — users could pair up and see each other's streaks. If your partner broke a streak, you got a gentle notification.

This single feature:
- **Increased D30 retention by 40%**
- **Drove organic word-of-mouth** (each user invited 1.8 friends on average)
- **Reduced churn by 25%** for paired users vs solo users

## Tech Stack

- **React Native** with Expo
- **Railway** for backend (Node.js + PostgreSQL)
- **BullMQ** for job queues
- **RevenueCat** for subscriptions
- **TikTok-native deep links** for viral sharing

## What He'd Do Differently

1. **Build for scale from day one.** "You don't need microservices, but at least have a plan for what happens if you get 10x traffic."
2. **Charge from the start.** "I left money on the table for 6 months because I was afraid to charge."
3. **Focus on one social feature early.** "The accountability partners feature should have been in v1."

## Pivot #1: The Gym Check-In Tool

Jake's first idea was simple: an app that let you check in at the gym and track your attendance. Think Foursquare, but for fitness.

"I thought gamifying gym visits would be enough motivation. Turns out, people who already go to the gym don't need motivation to go. And people who don't go to the gym don't download gym apps."

The app got 2,000 downloads in the first month. 90% of users opened it once and never came back.

## Pivot #2: The Workout Logger

Jake noticed the 10% who stuck around were all doing the same thing — manually logging their workouts in the notes section of the check-in. So he built a proper workout logging feature.

This was better. D7 retention jumped from 5% to 28%. But the market was brutally competitive. Strong, Hevy, and a dozen other apps already owned this space.

"I was competing with teams of 20 people on features I couldn't match as a solo dev. I needed a different angle."

## The Insight That Changed Everything

Jake started doing user interviews. One pattern kept emerging: people didn't want to work out alone. They had gym buddies, but coordinating schedules was chaos — group chats, DMs, missed messages.

> "Every single user I talked to said some version of 'I wish my friends could see my workouts and we could hold each other accountable.' That was the lightbulb." — Jake Morrison

## Pivot #3: Social Fitness Platform

Jake rebuilt the app around a core loop:
1. **Log your workout** (simplified — just exercise, sets, reps)
2. **Share to your feed** (automatic, no extra steps)
3. **React and comment** on friends' workouts
4. **Weekly challenges** between friend groups

The social features created a retention flywheel. When you finish a workout, your friends get a notification. When they react, you feel accountable to show up tomorrow.

Key metrics after the social pivot:
- **D7 retention**: 28% → 52%
- **D30 retention**: 11% → 38%
- **Average sessions per week**: 2.1 → 4.7
- **Organic invite rate**: 0.3 → 2.4 invites per user

## Monetization

Jake's premium tier ($7.99/month) unlocks:
- Advanced analytics (strength progression charts, volume tracking)
- Custom challenges (create your own instead of using presets)
- Priority support and feature requests

The social features are free. "That's the growth engine. You can't charge for the thing that makes people invite their friends."

Current MRR: $11,400, growing 15% month-over-month.

## The Technical Debt Problem

Three pivots meant three layers of technical debt. Jake spent two months doing a full rewrite before the social launch.

"I should have done incremental refactoring instead. The rewrite almost killed the project — two months of zero new features, and users started churning."

His advice: never do a full rewrite. Strangle the old code gradually.

## Tech Stack

- **React Native** with Expo
- **Supabase** for backend and real-time features
- **Expo Notifications** for push
- **RevenueCat** for subscriptions
- **Cloudflare R2** for workout photo storage

## Lessons From Three Pivots

1. **Ship fast, learn fast.** Each pivot took less time: 3 months, 6 weeks, 3 weeks.
2. **Listen to behavior, not words.** Users said they wanted gamification. They actually wanted connection.
3. **Social features are a superpower for retention.** But only if they're integrated into the core loop, not bolted on.
4. **Don't rewrite. Refactor.** The two-month rewrite was the biggest mistake.
5. **Charge for premium, not for growth.** Keep the viral loop free.

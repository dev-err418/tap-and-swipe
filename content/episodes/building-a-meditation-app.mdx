## The Origin Story

Sarah had been meditating for years, but every app she tried felt either too corporate or too new-age. "I wanted something that felt like a friend gently reminding you to breathe," she tells me.

She started building Stillness as a side project while working full-time at a fintech startup. The first version was embarrassingly simple — a timer with a calming gradient background.

## Finding Product-Market Fit

The first 1,000 users came from a single Reddit post in r/meditation. But it wasn't the post itself that drove growth — it was the comment section.

> "I kept replying to every single comment for three days straight. People started DM-ing me feature requests. That was my first real feedback loop." — Sarah Chen

She noticed a pattern: people didn't want more guided meditations. They wanted accountability. Someone to notice if they skipped a day.

## The Retention Breakthrough

Sarah's big insight was treating meditation streaks like a game — but without the guilt. Instead of showing a broken streak, Stillness shows a "total days meditated" counter that only goes up.

Key metrics after the change:
- **D1 retention**: 78% → 85%
- **D7 retention**: 45% → 92%
- **D30 retention**: 22% → 61%

## Monetization: From Ads to Subscriptions

The first monetization attempt was banner ads. Revenue was $3/day. Sarah knew she needed a different approach.

The subscription model she landed on:
1. **Free tier**: 5-minute daily meditation, streak tracking
2. **Premium ($6.99/mo)**: Unlimited session lengths, sleep sounds, progress insights
3. **Annual ($49.99/yr)**: Everything in Premium + offline downloads

The key was making the free tier genuinely useful. "If your free tier sucks, your conversion rate will too," Sarah says.

## The Push Notification Tweak

This is the part that blew my mind. Sarah A/B tested push notification copy:

**Version A** (old): "Time to meditate! Open Stillness to start your session."
**Version B** (new): "You've meditated 12 days this month. Ready for day 13?"

Version B doubled weekly active users. The difference? It reminded people of their progress instead of telling them what to do.

## Tech Stack

- **React Native** with Expo for cross-platform
- **RevenueCat** for subscription management
- **Firebase** for analytics and push notifications
- **Supabase** for the backend
- Custom audio engine built on `expo-av`

## Lessons Learned

1. **Ship the embarrassingly simple version first.** Sarah's timer-with-a-gradient got her first 1,000 users.
2. **Retention beats acquisition.** A 10% improvement in D7 retention compounds faster than a 10% increase in downloads.
3. **Talk to users every single day.** Sarah still does 3 user interviews per week.
4. **Your free tier is your marketing.** Make it genuinely useful.

## What's Next for Stillness

Sarah is working on a social feature — meditation circles where friends can meditate together in real-time. "It sounds counterintuitive for a meditation app, but loneliness is the #1 reason people stop meditating."

She's also exploring Apple Watch integration and plans to launch an Android version by Q3.

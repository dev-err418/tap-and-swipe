## Leaving Google

Yuki spent four years as a senior engineer at Google working on Search. She loved the technical challenges but felt disconnected from the end users.

"I wanted to build something where I could see the direct impact on people's lives. Language learning felt personal — I'm Japanese-American, and I saw how hard it was for my friends to learn Japanese with existing apps."

## The Problem With Existing Apps

Yuki's thesis was simple: most language learning apps teach you *about* a language. They don't teach you *to speak* it.

Her app, Kotoha, focuses on three things:
1. **Conversation-first approach** — every lesson is a realistic dialogue
2. **Custom spaced repetition** — adapted for Japanese's three writing systems
3. **Speech recognition** — practice speaking, not just reading

## Building the Spaced Repetition Engine

The spaced repetition algorithm was the hardest part. Standard SM-2 didn't work well for Japanese because kanji, hiragana, and vocabulary have very different forgetting curves.

Yuki built a modified algorithm that:
- Treats kanji recognition and kanji recall as separate skills
- Adjusts intervals based on word frequency in real Japanese media
- Groups related vocabulary to reinforce neural pathways

The result: users retained 73% more vocabulary after 30 days compared to standard spaced repetition.

## The App Store Featuring

Eight months after launch, Yuki got an email from Apple's editorial team. They wanted to feature Kotoha in the "Apps We Love" section.

> "I literally screamed. Then I spent the next 48 hours frantically fixing bugs and polishing the onboarding. You do NOT want to be featured with a buggy app." — Yuki Tanaka

The featuring drove 120,000 downloads in one week. But more importantly, it established credibility that kept organic downloads high for months afterward.

## Monetization

Kotoha uses a freemium model:
- **Free**: First 10 lessons, basic vocabulary review
- **Premium ($9.99/mo or $69.99/yr)**: Full course, AI conversation practice, offline mode

Key metrics:
- **Free-to-paid conversion**: 8.2% (well above the 2-3% industry average)
- **Annual-to-monthly ratio**: 60/40 in favor of annual
- **Current MRR**: $15,200

The high conversion rate comes from a deliberate "aha moment" in lesson 7 — where users first have a simulated conversation with an AI tutor. By that point, they're hooked.

## Managing Solo Founder Burnout

Yuki is candid about the mental health challenges. "Month 4 was the worst. Downloads were flat, I was burning through savings, and I kept comparing myself to funded teams with 10 engineers."

Her strategies for sustainability:
1. **Hard stop at 6 PM every day.** No exceptions.
2. **One feature per sprint.** Two-week sprints, no scope creep.
3. **Weekly user calls.** Hearing someone say "your app helped me order food in Tokyo" is the best anti-burnout medicine.
4. **Monthly financial reviews.** Knowing exactly when runway runs out removes ambiguity.

## Tech Stack

- **Swift** + SwiftUI (iOS only for now)
- **CloudKit** for sync
- **Core ML** for on-device speech recognition
- **Custom spaced repetition engine** in Swift
- **RevenueCat** for subscriptions

## What's Next

Yuki is working on Mandarin Chinese and Korean versions. She's also exploring a B2B play — corporate Japanese training for companies with teams in Tokyo.

"I want to prove that a solo founder can build a sustainable, profitable education app without VC money. So far, the numbers say yes."
